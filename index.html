SESSION 1 Introduction to Computer Vision What CV is, why it's hard (viewpoint, illumination, occlusion…), applications, and how images are represented. Concepts SESSION 2 Digital Image Fundamentals Image formation, sampling & quantization, spatial/intensity resolution, and intensity transforms (negative, log, gamma, contrast stretch). ConceptsDemoNumericals SESSION 3 Low-Level Vision & Color Histogram equalization, spatial filtering (convolution vs correlation), box vs Gaussian smoothing, and color spaces (RGB, CMYK, HSI, YCbCr). DemoNumericals SESSION 7 SIFT & RANSAC Scale-space, Difference-of-Gaussians, keypoints & descriptors that survive scale/rotation — and robust fitting with RANSAC. What the exam looks like

✦ From the Session 8 revision slide

The instructor stated the pattern directly: 4–5 questions of unequal weightage, drawn across all content covered so far, and a mix of theory and numericals with more focus on numerical understanding. That means the highest-leverage prep is being fast and correct on the worked problems — which is exactly what the Numericals and Exam Prep sections drill.

How to use this site

① Read for intuition

Start each topic with the Intuition callouts. If you can explain the idea in one sentence, the formula will stick.

② Play the demos

Drag a kernel across an image, watch Hough votes pile up, step through Canny. Seeing it move beats memorizing.

③ Re-derive numericals

Cover the solution, try it, then check. Speed on these is what the midsem rewards.
